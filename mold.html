Mould Risk Scoring and Standards

Overview

This document explains how to calculate a time-weighted Mould Risk Score based on temperature and relative humidity (RH). It references widely accepted thresholds from ASHRAE, ISO 13788, UK HSE/HHSRS, and EN standards. [cite: 1, 2, 3]

1. Key RH Thresholds for Mould

<60% RH: Low (Green)
60-69% RH: Caution (Amber)
≥70% RH: High (Red)

Mould typically requires sustained periods of >=70% RH to grow actively. [cite: 3, 4] Below ~60%, mould growth is very unlikely. [cite: 4]

UK HSE highlights >70% as a key driver of damp and mould. [cite: 4, 5] ASHRAE guidelines limit comfortable indoor RH to ~60% max; beyond ~70%, mould risk rises quickly. [cite: 5, 6] ISO 13788 references 80% surface humidity (which usually means ~70% air RH at normal temps). [cite: 6, 7]

2. Time-Weighted Mould Score

A simple points system factors in both severity and duration of high RH: [cite: 7]

RH Range Points
RH < 60% 0
60% <= RH < 70% 1
70% <= RH < 80% 2
RH >= 80% 3

If the temperature is <16Â°C, add +1 point (risk of condensation on cold surfaces). [cite: 7, 8] Each sensor reading yields some points; sum over a day or week. Prolonged or more extreme humidity accumulates more points. [cite: 8, 9, 10]

3. Classification Example

Classification Points
Low (Green) Daily total below ~20
Medium (Amber) ~20-40 points
High (Red) 40+ points or continuous 24h over 70%

4. References

ASHRAE: 30-60% recommended for health/comfort; ~70% is a typical danger level. Standard 160 warns against >80% surface RH. [cite: 11]

ISO 13788: 80% surface RH limit, ~70% air RH. [cite: 11]

EN 15251 / EN 16798: Usually <70% RH indoors for comfort. [cite: 11]

UK HHSRS / HSE: Damp & mould recognized as category hazards; keep below 70% to prevent growth. [cite: 11]

5. Implementation Steps

  1. Collect Temp & RH readings regularly (every 10 or 15 minutes). [cite: 11, 12]
  2. Calculate points as above for each reading; sum daily/weekly. [cite: 12, 13]
  3. Flag High if the total is above the threshold or if RH >=70% persists 24+ hours. [cite: 13, 14]
  4. Display final classification (e.g., RAG: Green, Amber, Red). [cite: 14]

6. Summary

<60% = safe, >=70% = high risk. [cite: 15, 16] Time-weighted approach ensures prolonged or severe humidity is highlighted. [cite: 16] Aligns with ASHRAE, ISO 13788, and UK HHSRS guidelines.
